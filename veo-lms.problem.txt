Cost Optimization (Extremely Important)
Real-world software is not only about features.
It is also about cost.
One of the evaluation criteria will be how efficiently you design and operate the system.
Think about:
Hosting costs
Storage costs
Video delivery costs
Processing costs
Database costs
Bandwidth costs
I encourage you to explore cost-effective solutions.
Examples:
Cloudflare R2
Cloudflare CDN
Cloudflare Workers
BunnyCDN
Backblaze B2
Serverless architectures
Edge-based solutions
Avoid expensive architectures unless they are justified.
For example:
If video processing is required, think about processing videos temporarily and shutting down resources afterwards instead of keeping expensive servers running continuously.
A system costing ₹500 per month may be more impressive than a system costing ₹5000 per month if both achieve similar outcomes.
In your submission, explain:
Estimated monthly cost
Services used
Why you selected those services
Trade-offs considered

Final Selection Process
There will NOT be a separate Node.js interview.
There will NOT be a separate TypeScript interview.
Instead, your project itself will become your interview.
If your project is shortlisted, I will personally schedule a WhatsApp call with you.
Expected duration:
Approximately 1 to 1.5 hours.
During this discussion, I will ask you to explain:
What you built
Why you built it that way
Why you selected a particular architecture
Why you selected a particular database
Why you selected particular cloud services
How your authentication works
How your authorization works
How your payment flow works
How your deployment works
How your security works
I may ask technical cross-questions throughout the discussion.
You should be able to explain your own project confidently.
If you simply generated code using AI without understanding it, that will become obvious during the discussion.
Using AI is encouraged.
Blindly depending on AI is not.
Final selection will depend heavily on your ability to explain and justify your decisions.
Contribution, Recognition & Compensation
VeoLMS is an open-source learning project, and selected core team members will be contributing as part of an open-source collaboration.
This is not a paid internship, job, or salaried role. There will be no fixed salary, stipend, or monetary compensation for being part of the core team.
However, after the first production version of VeoLMS is successfully released, active core team members who contribute meaningfully to the project will receive a Contribution Certificate and/or a Letter of Recommendation from my side.
This recognition can be used to showcase your real-world open-source contribution, teamwork, engineering experience, and involvement in building a production-level LMS platform to future employers, clients, and collaborators.
Please note that this recognition will be given only to selected core team members who actively participate, take ownership, contribute consistently, and help ship the first version of VeoLMS to production.
